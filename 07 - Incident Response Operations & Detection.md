# 🛡️ Incident Response Operations & Detection

This document captures my learning on incident response processes, detection tools, and security operations concepts.

---

## 🔹 Overview

Incident response is the structured approach used to identify, manage, and recover from cybersecurity incidents. It involves coordination, communication, and documentation to minimize damage and restore normal operations.

---

## 🔹 Incident Response Lifecycle (NIST)

The NIST Incident Response Lifecycle includes:

- Preparation  
- Detection and Analysis  
- Containment, Eradication, and Recovery  
- Post-Incident Activity  

---

## 🔹 Incident Handling & Documentation

- Incident handler’s journal is used to record actions taken during an incident  
- Documentation ensures accountability and supports investigation  
- Final reports summarize findings, impact, and response actions  

---

## 🔹 Incident Response Teams

- CSIRT (Computer Security Incident Response Team) handles incidents  
- Roles include analysts, incident handlers, and coordinators  
- Effective communication is critical during incident response  

---

## 🔹 Incident Response Planning

- Incident response plans define procedures and responsibilities  
- Playbooks provide step-by-step guidance for specific incidents  
- Types of playbooks include malware response, phishing response, and data breach response  

---

## 🔹 Detection & Monitoring

### Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)
- Monitor network or system activities for malicious behavior  

### Detection Tools
- SIEM (Security Information and Event Management)  
- SOAR (Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response)  

### Key Concepts
- Alert and event management  
- Automation for detecting anomalies  
- Crowd-sourced threat intelligence  

---

## 🔹 Triage

- Process of prioritizing incidents based on severity  
- Helps allocate resources efficiently  

---

## 🔹 Chain of Custody

- Tracks evidence handling during an investigation  
- Ensures integrity of evidence  
- Broken chain of custody can invalidate findings  

---

## 🔹 Threat Detection & Indicators

### Pyramid of Pain
- Framework showing how difficult it is for attackers to change indicators  

### Indicators of Compromise (IoCs)
- Signs that a system has been breached  
- Examples:
  - Unusual network traffic  
  - Suspicious file activity  
  - Unauthorized access attempts  

### CI/CD Pipeline Risks
- Compromised code or unauthorized changes  
- Malicious injections in deployment processes  

---

## 🔹 Network & Security Operations

- Network traffic monitoring helps detect suspicious activity  
- Command and Control (C2) servers are used by attackers to control compromised systems  

### Security Teams
- SOC (Security Operations Center): monitors and responds to threats  
- NOC (Network Operations Center): manages network performance  

---

## 🔹 Business Continuity

- Ensures critical operations continue during incidents  
- Includes backup systems and recovery strategies
