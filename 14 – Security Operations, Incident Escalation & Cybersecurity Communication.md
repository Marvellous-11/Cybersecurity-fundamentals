Overview

This project documents my learning experience in security operations, incident escalation, asset protection, stakeholder communication, and cybersecurity reporting as part of the Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate on Coursera.

In cybersecurity, security analysts must identify threats, classify sensitive data, recognize incidents, protect organizational assets, and communicate effectively with stakeholders. This project highlights essential concepts used in SOC (Security Operations Center) environments and real-world cybersecurity operations.

Stakeholders in Cybersecurity
Understanding Stakeholders

A stakeholder is any person or group affected by cybersecurity decisions or incidents.

Five Key Stakeholders

Risk Management Team
Identifies and manages organizational risks
Helps reduce security vulnerabilities

Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
Oversees overall business operations
Makes executive-level decisions

Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
Manages organizational finances
Evaluates financial impact of incidents

Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
Leads the organization’s cybersecurity strategy
Oversees security operations and policies

Operations Manager
Ensures daily business operations continue efficiently
Coordinates operational responses during incidents
